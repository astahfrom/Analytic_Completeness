\title{Completeness of $Q_0$}
\author{Asta Halkjær Boserup\and Jonathan Juli\'an Huerta y Munive\and Anders Schlichtkrul}
\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
  We formalize the completeness of Peter B. Andrews' system $Q_0$ for Higher-Order Logic 
  following his textbook ``An Introduction to Mathematical Logic and Type Theory: To Truth Through Proof''. 
  The formalization builds on D\'iaz's formalization of $Q_0$'s syntax, semantics, soundness and consistency. 
  The completeness is with respect to general models. We prove completeness by introducing an abstract 
  consistency property for $Q_0$ and using the framework for abstract consistency properties by From and 
  Schlichtkrull to get a model existence theorem for $Q_0$. In our application of the framework we also adapt
  proofs from Andrews' book to this context.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
We give a completeness proof of Peter B. Andrews' system $Q_0$ for Higher-Order Logic following 
his textbook ``An Introduction to Mathematical Logic and Type Theory: To Truth Through Proof' 
\cite{andrews2013introduction}. 
We build the completeness proof on D\'iaz's \cite{Q0_Metatheory-AFP} formalization of $Q_0$, and thus 
also Paulson's Zermelo Fraenkel Set Theory in Higher-Order Logic \cite{ZFC_in_HOL-AFP}. D\'iaz's formalization is
combined with the framework by From and Schlichtkrull for abstract consistency properties \cite{Abstract_Consistency_Property-AFP,DBLP:conf/itp/FromS25}.
This framework fits Andrews' completeness proof better than other frameworks for logics
such as the framework 
for synthetic completeness \cite{Synthetic_Completeness-AFP,DBLP:conf/cpp/From25}, 
for Beth/Hintikka style completeness proofs \cite{Abstract_Completeness-AFP,DBLP:conf/cade/Blanchette0T14,DBLP:journals/jar/BlanchettePT17},
and for saturation based provers \cite{Splitting_Framework-AFP,DBLP:journals/jar/WaldmannTRB22,DBLP:conf/cade/EbnerBT21,DBLP:conf/cpp/TourretB21,Saturation_Framework_Extensions-AFP,DBLP:conf/cade/WaldmannTRB20}.

There are two formalizations of $Q_0$ in Isabelle, but they do not cover completeness.
Specifically there is D\'iaz's \cite{Q0_Metatheory-AFP} formalization of $Q_0$ on 
which this theory is built and Schlichtkrull's formalization of $Q_0$
completeness \cite{Q0_Soundness-AFP}.
Formalizations of other systems for higher-order logic also do not cover completeness.
There is specifically Arthan's specification of HOL in ProofPower \cite{arthan1993,arthan1991sem,arthan1991ded}, John Harrison's formalization of the proof system of HOL Light in HOL Light \cite{DBLP:conf/cade/Harrison06} and the formalization by Kumar et al.\ HOL Light with definitions and a verified implementation in HOL4\cite{DBLP:conf/itp/KumarAMO14,DBLP:journals/jar/KumarAMO16,DBLP:conf/itp/AbrahamssonMKS22,DBLP:conf/itp/AbrahamssonM23}.
Another formalization with the focus on implementation is by
Ro{\ss}kopf and Nipkow \cite{DBLP:conf/cade/NipkowR21,Metalogic_ProofChecker-AFP,DBLP:journals/jar/RosskopfN23} who in Isabelle/HOL give both a
formalization of a proof system for terms in Isabelle's metalogic and a formalization of an implementation
of a checker and prove that the checker implements the system. 
Additionally there are works formalizing the metatheory of the calculus of constructions and
the calculus of inductive constructions \cite{DBLP:conf/types/Barras96,barras:inria-00073667,coqincoqmanuscript,coqincoqcontribs,DBLP:journals/pacmpl/SozeauBFTW20}.

The only other formalizations of completeness with general models we know are for second-order logic
by Kock and Kirst \cite{DBLP:conf/cpp/KochK22} in Coq and by From and Schlichtkrull in Isabelle/HOL \cite{Abstract_Consistency_Property-AFP,DBLP:conf/itp/FromS25}.
